=== STEP BY STEP SKYBOX PIPELINE

Open \Desktop\spacescape\Spacescape.exe

Click "New Layer"

*** STARS

First we'll add in one or more layers of background stars. 
Open the layer and name it "Stars00" or whatever.
Keep the type "points" for random points.
Pick a random seed
Number of Points Put in something between 10K and 100K
Point Size can/should be bigger than one pixel. Try 3 or 4.

Consider a couple more star layers with different colors. Change color with "Near Color". Check this out for inspiration:
https://neal.fun/constellation-draw/

I don't really use the "mask" features with the background stars.

*** BIGGER STARS

Now add a new layer and change "Layer Type" to "billboard"
This is for random images scattered throughout.
Though you can set it to one image, no matter what I try, other layers are on top of it so this is NOT how to put a big planet on or whatever.

Set the image to billboard at the bottom in "Billboard Texture".
Pick a star image to billboard from this folder:
\spacescape\media\materials\textures\
or just look online for PNGs.

Set "Number of Billboards" to something very low (like 1 or 2) if your image is largish.
You can change billboard size with "Near Billboard Size" and "Far Billboard Size". Both change the size. I don't understand the difference.

You can also scatter LOTS of small star PNGs using billboard. In the all_features example I billboarded flare-green1.png 1000 times at near size 0.01 and far size 0.05

In point and billboard, you can enable "Mask" then set a mask random seed, noise to "ridged", and so on to only show stars in certain locations. I did this to the green stars. I don't notice much of an effect, but I think it makes the green stars appear only in noisy chains like how the nebulae below work, but I'm not sure it's worth it.

*** NEBULAE

Create a new layer and start with these settings:
Layer type: noise
Noise type: Ridged
Octaves 5
Power: 0.04
Change the inner color, but not the outer.

You can add a few nebulae.

Finally export to high res png. (Exporting for Unity has been fine for me.)

Close Spacescape.exe. That's all I know how to do with it.

*** BIG PLANETS, STARS, OR OTHER IMAGES

Find a royalty free image of some kind. For this example I used this (which may or may not be free):
https://www.space.com/jupiter-moon-io-juno-probe-photos-october-2023

In Krita I used the ellipse selection tool to cut out just the planet as accurately as I could.
Then I right clicked on the selection mask in the far right panel with the layers and such and selected
	Convert -> Convert to transparency mask

After exporting to PNG, there were sill little white pixels on the edges so I got rid of those with this
https://onlinepngtools.com/refine-png-edges

Open up one of the the final exported Spacescape.exe images in krita and drag and drop the cutout image in as a new layer. Position and size to taste. Applying a filter can be fun. Play around with
	Filter -> Adjust -> Color Balance
to change the temperature of the image.
	Filter -> Adjust -> Color Adjustment Curve
is also a neat one.
Then save/export back over the original image.

*** FINAL PROCESSING

Lastly go here:
https://danilw.github.io/GLSL-howto/cubemap_to_panorama_js/cubemap_to_panorama.html
Set ALL rotation degrees to zero then click update.
Uncheck the "apply Mipmap" setting otherwise the image will be blurry.
Change export resolution to 7680 x 4320
Upload each image into the corresponding front/back up/down left/right button.
Then click "Download(save) result"

Rename the panorama image sent to downloads then drop it into Godot assets folder.
